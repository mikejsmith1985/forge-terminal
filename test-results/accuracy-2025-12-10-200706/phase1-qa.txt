Q1: How do I enable session logging in Forge?
Response:
 To enable session logging in Forge Terminal, you can use the Artificial Memory (AM) logging feature. Here's how:

1. Open Forge Terminal if it's not already open.
2. Click on the gear icon located at the bottom left corner of the terminal window to access settings.
3. In the settings panel, navigate to the "Logging" section.
4. Toggle on the switch for "AM Logging" to enable session logging.
5. You can customize your logging preferences such as log file location, log retention, and screenshot frequency in the same section.
6. After enabling AM Logging, all your terminal sessions will be logged, including text output and screenshots.
